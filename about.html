About Me

I’m Drew Kazanis, a server and builder at heart and a former wildland firefighter from rural Spokane, Washington. Fresh out of high school, I spent five seasons on the fireline, earning my FFT1 “Squad Boss” certification and logging over 400 days on some of the largest wildfires in U.S. history. Leading crews of up to a dozen men across our national forests while working toward my associate’s degree at Walla Walla Community College taught me discipline, leadership under pressure, and adaptability.

Alongside firefighting, I discovered my passion for hospitality behind the bars and on the busy floors at No-Li Brewing, Plumb Cellars, Locust Cider, and more. Those thousands of hours sharpened my timing, clear communication, and human centered service in fast-paced, customer-focused environments;

I always aim to be the person I’d want serving me.

I landed in EWU’s design program driven by a “let’s make things” attitude. Gaining all these new skills reinforced what I already knew: persistence and common sense are what shape every idea into something tangible.
