About Me

I’m Drew Kazanis,

a server and builder at heart—and a former wildland firefighter from rural Spokane, Washington. Fresh out of high school, I spent five seasons on the fireline—earning my FFT1 “Squad Boss” certification, spending over 400 days working the largest wildfires in U.S. history, while also earning my associate’s degree at Walla Walla Community College.

Demanding coursework, hectic bar shifts, firefighting missions, and winery tasting-room events, honed my discipline, leadership under pressure, and adaptability.

Alongside firefighting, I spent I found love in the service industry; behind bars and on busy floors at No-Li Brewing, Plumb Cellars, Locust Cider, and more. Thousands of hours sharpened my instincts for timing, clear communication, and delivering honest, human centered service in fast-paced, customer focused environments.

Behind bar rails and busy beer halls, I’ve always made it my mission to be the person I’d want serving me.

I found myself in EWU’s Design program driven by “let’s make things.”

Gaining so many great skills at EWU, I also learned that persistence and common sense shape every idea into something tangible.
