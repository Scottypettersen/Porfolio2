I’m Drew Kazanis, a builder at heart and a former wildland firefighter from rural Spokane, Washington. Fresh out of high school, I jumped into five seasons on the fireline—earning my FFT1 “Squad Boss” certification, logging over 400 days on some of the largest wildfires in U.S. history, and finishing my associate degree at Walla Walla Community College along the way. Those experiences taught me discipline, leadership under pressure, and how to adapt on the fly.

Alongside firefighting, I spent six years immersed in the service industry—behind bars and on busy floors at No-Li Brewing, Plumb Cellars, and Locust Cider. Those thousands of hours sharpened my instincts for timing, clear communication, and delivering honest, human-centered service in fast-paced environments.

I found my creative home at Eastern Washington University’s Design program after pitching my “let’s build something” mentality to an advisor. Courses like Drawing for Communication pushed me outside my comfort zone and showed me that persistence pays off when you bring ideas to life—sometimes feeling like you’ve stitched together your own Frankenstein.

Now, as I wrap up my degree, I focus on interaction and product design—crafting tools, visuals, and systems that solve real problems and tell meaningful stories. I’m fluent in Figma, Illustrator, Photoshop, and I’m continually expanding my 3D-modeling and system-design skills. My north star is simple: build what works, build what lasts, and build what feels honest—and help others do the same.
